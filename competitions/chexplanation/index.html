A Chest X-Ray Segmentation Dataset And Competition.

What is CheXplanation?

CheXplanation is a radiologist-annotated segmentation dataset on chest x-rays and competition for automated pathology segmentation. The dataset can also be used for evaluation of x-ray interpretation models.

Read the Paper (Saporta et al.)

Why CheXplanation?

Chest radiography is the most common imaging examination globally, and is critical for screening, diagnosis, and management of many life threatening diseases. While deep learning has enabled automated medical imaging interpretation at a level shown to surpass that of practicing experts, the “black box” nature of neural networks represents a barrier to physicians’ trust and model adoption in the clinical setting. Therefore to promote clinical adoption it is crucial for the model to be able to accurately identify regions and boundaries of pathologies in addition to generate probabilistic outputs. For progress in both development and validation of automated segmentation, we published a new radiologist-annotated segmentation dataset and established expert human performance benchmark.

How can I participate?

CheXplanation uses a hidden test set with official evaluation of models. Teams submit their segmentations per pathology which will be compared against the reference segmentations. mIoU (mean intersection over union) will be used as the evaluation metric.

How did we produce the CheXplanation dataset?

The original chest x-rays came from CheXpert, a large public dataset for chest X-ray interpretation. We provided pixel-level reference segmentations on the CheXpert validation and test set from two board-certified radiologists who were asked to segment any of the 10 pathologies that were present in each CXR as determined by the dataset’s ground-truth labels. We also established a human benchmark by collecting segmentations from three additional radiologists who were asked to segment the 10 pathologies of interest present in each CXR as determined by the dataset’s ground-truth labels (there was no overlap between these three radiologists and the two who drew the reference segmentations). The ten competition tasks are: Airspace Opacity, Atelectasis, Cardiomegaly, Consolidation, Edema, Enlarged Cardiomediastinum, Lung Lesion, Pleural Effusion, Pneumothorax, and Support Devices.

Validation and Test Sets

The CheXpert validation set consists of 234 chest X-rays from 200 patients randomly sampled from the full dataset and was labeled according to the consensus of three board-certified radiologists. The test set consists of 668 chest X-rays from 500 patients not included in the training or validation sets and was labeled according to the consensus of five board-certified radiologists. 

We provided the reference segmentations and corresponding labels of the validation set. The test set will be used as a held-out set for leaderboard evaluation and will only include the original chest x-ray images.
